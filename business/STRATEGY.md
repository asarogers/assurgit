# Assurgit — Business Strategy & Intelligence Document

## 1. What Assurgit Is

Assurgit is a **done-for-you AI video content service**. We use the client's HeyGen avatar and voice clone to produce branded short-form videos, then automatically publish them to Instagram, TikTok, LinkedIn, and YouTube on a weekly cadence.

The client never films again after the initial setup call. Everything — research, scripting, rendering, QC, and publishing — is handled by the Assurgit system.

---

## 2. Business Model

**Revenue model:** Monthly subscription (SaaS-style, month-to-month)

**Core value exchange:** The client pays for content velocity + brand consistency + zero operational burden.

**Key output:** 7 published videos per week, per client, on 4 platforms.

**Positioning:** Not a template tool, not an editing service, not a freelancer. A fully autonomous system that runs on their face and voice.

---

## 3. System Architecture

### Infrastructure
- **Backend:** OpenClaw + PostgreSQL + File-Tree workflow
- **Avatar/Voice:** HeyGen API (avatar clone + voice clone)
- **Distribution:** Platform APIs (Instagram, TikTok, LinkedIn, YouTube)
- **Memory layer:** Per-client intelligence store tracking what works

### Research Layer (runs weekly per client)
- **Competitive research:** Monitor competitor content and positioning
- **Content research:** Track trending topics in the client's niche
- **Distribution research:** Identify optimal formats per platform per week
- **Search intent research:** Capture keyword and search-driven content opportunities

### Content Pipeline (weekly cadence)
| Day | Action |
|-----|--------|
| Monday | Scripts generated and sent to client for approval |
| Tuesday–Wednesday | 48-hour client review window (optional) |
| Saturday | Video rendering (HeyGen) |
| Sunday | Videos scheduled across all platforms |
| Monday | Videos go live — client wakes up with 7 new pieces of content |

### Client Relationship Layer
- **Friday strategy call:** Weekly call with auto-generated briefing covering last week's performance, competitive intel, and next week's content plan
- **Email delivery (Starter):** MP4 files delivered via email for manual posting
- **Memory system:** The system tracks which hooks, formats, topics, and CTAs perform best per client and continuously improves

## 5. Target Client Profiles (Priority Order)

**Profile:** Coaches, consultants, speakers doing $200K–$2M/year ARR
**Pain:** They know video works. They just can't film consistently.
**Why they buy:** They want to look prolific on social without filming. The avatar + voice clone is the perfect solution.
**Channel:** LinkedIn DMs, cold email, YouTube ads targeting "content for coaches"
**Price sensitivity:** Low — they already invest in high-ticket marketing

### Priority 2: B2B SaaS Founders / Solo CEOs
**Profile:** Founders of $1M–$10M ARR SaaS companies, usually active on LinkedIn
**Pain:** They want thought leadership content but have no time to produce it
**Why they buy:** Weekly LinkedIn presence without lifting a finger
**Channel:** LinkedIn DMs (search: "CEO" + "SaaS"), Twitter/X outreach
**Price sensitivity:** Medium — ROI-focused, need to see content performance data

### Priority 3: Agency Owners (White-Label)
**Profile:** Marketing agencies wanting to resell video content as a service
**Pain:** They can't fulfill video at scale for clients
**Why they buy:** Plug the Scale tier into their agency stack and resell at $8K–$15K/month
**Channel:** Agency owner communities, cold outreach to marketing agency owners
**Price sensitivity:** Low on Scale — high margin resale opportunity

### Priority 4: Real Estate Team Leaders & Financial Advisors
**Profile:** RE team leaders with 5–20 agents, fee-based financial advisors with $50M+ AUM
**Pain:** Compliance-friendly content is hard. Video consistency is impossible.
**Why they buy:** Content machine that maintains their personal brand while they close deals
**Channel:** RE/finance-specific Facebook groups, referral from CRMs, industry events
**Price sensitivity:** Medium — results-driven, needs trust signals

### Priority 5: E-Commerce Brand Founders
**Profile:** DTC founders doing $1M–$5M/year with an identifiable founder brand
**Pain:** UGC content is expensive and inconsistent
**Why they buy:** Founder-led content at scale to drive top-of-funnel
**Channel:** Shopify communities, DTC Twitter/X, paid acquisition
**Price sensitivity:** Higher — product margins dictate ROI threshold

## 7. Acquisition Channels

### Channel 1: LinkedIn DMs (Primary — Near-Term)
**Approach:** Manual outreach to personal brand founders and solo CEOs
**Message angle:** "I see you're active on LinkedIn — do you have a system for video content, or is it still hit-or-miss?"
**Target:** Profiles with 2K+ followers who post infrequently
**Goal:** 5–10 conversations/week, 1–2 closes/month per outreach rep

### Channel 2: Cold Email (Secondary — Near-Term)
**Approach:** Apollo or Clay-sourced list of coaches/consultants with websites but no video presence
**Angle:** Pain-led — reference their lack of video and offer a sample avatar video
**Sequence:** 3 emails over 10 days
**Goal:** 2–3% reply rate → 1 demo booked per 50 contacts

### Channel 3: YouTube (Medium-Term — 60–90 days)
**Approach:** Build a YouTube channel showing "How I built a video content machine using AI"
**Content:** Behind-the-scenes of Assurgit system, client results, HeyGen tutorials
**Goal:** Inbound leads from people searching for AI video + personal brand content solutions

### Channel 4: Agency White-Label Program (Medium-Term)
**Approach:** Identify marketing agencies doing social media management
**Pitch:** "Add AI video as a service without hiring a team"
**Structure:** Scale tier resold at agency margins
**Goal:** 3–5 agency partners generating $5K–$15K/month each in pass-through revenue

### Channel 5: ProductHunt Launch (One-Time — Month 2–3)
**Approach:** Launch on ProductHunt as "AI video content service for personal brands"
**Goal:** 200+ upvotes, press coverage, 50+ trial signups

## 8. Weekly Production Cadence

### Monday
- Scripts generated from research pipeline
- Scripts emailed to client with 48-hour review window
- Previous week's videos go live on all platforms

### Tuesday–Wednesday
- Client review window (optional — can skip, content goes live as written)
- Revision requests accepted (1 round)

### Thursday
- Final scripts locked
- HeyGen render jobs queued

### Friday
- Strategy call with client (Growth + Scale tiers)
- Auto-generated briefing distributed: last week's metrics, competitor moves, next week's topic plan

### Saturday
- Video rendering complete (HeyGen batch processing)
- Human QC: review rendering quality, caption accuracy, branding, format specs

### Sunday
- Videos scheduled across all platforms via scheduling APIs
- Confirmation sent to client: "Your 7 videos are scheduled for this week"

## 9. Scaling Phases

### Phase 1: 0–10 Clients (Manual + Semi-Automated)
**Focus:** Prove the product, get testimonials, learn what content formats work per niche
**Operation:** Founder runs Friday calls, manages QC personally
**Revenue target:** $20K–$35K MRR
**Key milestone:** First 3 Growth clients retained for 90+ days

### Phase 2: 10–30 Clients (Systematize)
**Focus:** Build the client onboarding playbook, hire first content QC person
**Operation:** Hire a Friday call operator (CSM), systematize research pipeline
**Revenue target:** $70K–$105K MRR
**Key milestone:** Churn under 5%/month, NPS above 50

### Phase 3: 30–75 Clients (Scale)
**Focus:** Agency white-label program launch, paid acquisition
**Operation:** Dedicated account management, automated QC layer, agency portal
**Revenue target:** $200K–$400K MRR
**Key milestone:** First 3 white-label agency partners

### Phase 4: 75+ Clients (Systematized Growth)
**Focus:** Enterprise/team accounts, expand to more avatar seats per client
**Operation:** Full ops team, platform integrations, API access tier
**Revenue target:** $500K+ MRR
